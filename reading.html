The last thing I will be sharing with you is reading. Yes, yes, some may call it boring; and reading is technically just looking at the remains of a ground up tree and hallucinating, but I love it nonetheless. I like all finds of fiction, but Victorian era paranormal horror with a dash of LGBT+ romance will forever have a special place in my heart. To all you non-fiction lovers, I'll admit, reading autobiographies and biographies is cool, but still subpar. Recently, I've gotten into reading books online instead of a physical copies due to two reasons: COVID and laziness. I just don't have the time or energy to beg my parents to drive all the way to the library and have them stick around until near closing time, so I got into the habit of searching "read book name online free" into the Chrome search bar. I'll also download pdfs of these books when I want to read something offine, like on the bus or during a trip. A good site to use is u1lib.org. If you want to know if I've ever read those books (don't lie, you know what I mean), I shamefully admit, yes. I've collected an entire series of them, actually. Not my proudest moment, but hey, we gotta do what we gotta do.

Here's a list of those books in case you all are curious (and I know you are):
(btw the first three are the series I told you about)

  • The Maddest Obsession
